5 Simple SEO Tips for Small Businesses

Search engine optimization (SEO) can sound complicated, but the basics are straightforward. If you run a small business and want more people to find you through Google, these five tips are a great place to start. You do not need any technical background to begin using them today.

1. Use Clear Page Titles

Every page on your website should have a unique, descriptive title. The page title is one of the first things Google looks at when deciding what your page is about. It also appears as the clickable blue link in search results.

A good page title includes your main keyword and accurately describes the content on that page. For example, instead of a vague title like "Welcome," use something specific like "Affordable Plumbing Services in Austin, TX."

Keep your titles under 60 characters so they display fully in search results. For more about how titles and other elements help your website, read our guide on why every local business needs a website.

2. Write Helpful Meta Descriptions

A meta description is the short paragraph that appears below your page title in search results. While it does not directly affect rankings, a well-written meta description encourages people to click on your link instead of a competitor's.

Write meta descriptions that are clear, specific, and between 120 and 160 characters. Include your primary keyword naturally and give readers a reason to visit your page.

  • Summarize the page content in one or two sentences.
  • Include a call to action when appropriate (for example, "Learn more" or "Get a free quote").
  • Avoid duplicate descriptions across different pages.

3. Use Headings Properly

Headings (H1, H2, H3, and so on) help both readers and search engines understand the structure of your content. Use one H1 per page for the main topic, then H2 headings for major sections, and H3 headings for subsections.

Good heading structure makes your content easier to scan and helps Google identify what each section is about. Think of headings as an outline of your page.

4. Add Internal Links

Internal links are links from one page on your website to another page on the same website. They help visitors navigate your site and help search engines discover and understand all of your content.

When you mention a related topic in your content, link to the relevant page on your site. For example, if you write about building trust with customers, you might link to a post about how Google reviews help local SEO.

Use descriptive link text that tells readers what they will find when they click. Avoid generic phrases like "click here." Instead, make the linked text describe the destination page.

5. Keep Your Website Mobile-Friendly

More than half of all web traffic now comes from mobile devices. Google uses mobile-friendliness as a ranking factor, so a website that works well on phones and tablets will perform better in search results.

To make your website mobile-friendly:

  • Use a responsive design that adapts to different screen sizes.
  • Make sure text is large enough to read without zooming.
  • Keep buttons and links easy to tap with a finger.
  • Avoid pop-ups that cover the entire screen on mobile.

If you are not sure how your site looks on mobile, try Google's free Mobile-Friendly Test tool. A mobile-friendly website is not just good for SEO — it also creates a better experience for your customers.
